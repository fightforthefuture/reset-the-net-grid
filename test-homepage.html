How to #ResetTheNet

The problem

Governments are exploiting weak links in Internet security to spy on the entire world, twisting the Internet we love into something it was never meant to be.

The solution

We can't stop targeted attacks, but we *can* stop mass surveillance, by building proven security into the websites and apps people use everyday.

The plan

First, get hundreds of sites & apps to add proven security (like SSL). Then on June 5, we'll run a splash screen *everywhere* to spread NSA-resistant privacy tools.

What you can do, right now!

Websites

Pledge to add SSL, HSTS & PFS protection this year; it matters! Then, on June 5th, run the splash screen to promote free software for end-to-end encryption. Already rocking SSL & HSTS? Consider approaches to end-to-end crypto.

ADD YOUR SITE

Mobile apps

If your app talks to a server, use SSL & cert pinning, and make sure all third party code like ads & analytics does too. If it's a messaging app, consider end-to-end encryption. Then on June 5th, use your reach to promote a mobile privacy pack.

All of us

Pledge to try & share NSA-resistant privacy tools on June 5th, and join the Thunderclap to protest on social media. In the meantime, join the #ResetTheNet Twitter brigade & help get more sites to join in.

Wherever you are, whatever you do, there are concrete ways to win huge victories for privacy, right now.

More Ways to Participate

What can I do?

By June 5th, do something to reset your part of the web. What's the most you can do to block mass surveillance? For sites and apps, the first step is securing the connection between you and your users. For anyone with an audience, or a circle of friends, the best thing you can do is promote end-to-end encryption (there are some really easy-to-use tools for that now, but they need promotion). For visionary geeks, sky's the limit. Think big! :)

Add your website, company, or app.

How you participate is up to you, but be ambitious! Sites: promise to add HTTPS, HSTS, and PFS this year. Mobile apps: add SSL & cert pinning, including for third party code like ad networks and analytics. Large companies should follow the Encrypt all the Things Data Security Action Plan. Everyone else: promote the privacy pack on June 5th to spread privacy tools!
